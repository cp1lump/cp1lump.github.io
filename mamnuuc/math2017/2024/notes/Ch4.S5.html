4.5 Elementary properties of the Riemann integral

Theorem 4.16 is a convenient tool for establishing many useful properties of the Riemann integral.

Proposition 4.22

Let ff be Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b] and on [b,c][b,c]. Then ff is Riemann integrable on [a,c][a,c] and

∫acf=∫abf+∫bcf.\int_{a}^{c}f=\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{b}^{c}f.
Proof.

By Theorem 4.16, there exist sequences of dissections (𝒟n′)(\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}) of [a,b][a,b] and (𝒟n′′)(\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}) of [b,c][b,c] such that l𝒟n′⁢(f)→∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f, u𝒟n′⁢(f)→∫abfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f, l𝒟n′′⁢(f)→∫bcfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{b}^{c}f, and u𝒟n′′⁢(f)→∫bcfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{b}^{c}f. Let 𝒟n=𝒟n′∪𝒟n′′\mathscr{D}_{n}=\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}\cup\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}. Then 𝒟n\mathscr{D}_{n} is a dissection of [a,c][a,c] and it follows directly from Definition 4.6 that l𝒟n⁢(f)=l𝒟n′⁢(f)+l𝒟n′′⁢(f)l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)=l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(f)+l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{% \prime\prime}}(f) and u𝒟n⁢(f)=u𝒟n′⁢(f)+u𝒟n′′⁢(f)u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)=u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(f)+u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{% \prime\prime}}(f). Hence, by the Algebra of Limits, l𝒟n⁢(f)→∫abf+∫bcfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{b}^{c}f and u𝒟n⁢(f)→∫abf+∫bcfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{b}^{c}f, so the claim follows from Theorem 4.16. ∎

We next want to prove that the Riemann integral is linear, that is,

∫ab(α⁢f+β⁢g)=α⁢∫abf+β⁢∫abg,\int_{a}^{b}(\alpha f+\beta g)=\alpha\int_{a}^{b}f+\beta\int_{a}^{b}g,

for any constants α,β\alpha,\beta and any integrable functions f,gf,g. To do this, we need the following:

Lemma 4.23

Let f,g:[a,b]→ℝf,g:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} be bounded functions and 𝒟\mathscr{D} be a dissection of [a,b][a,b]. Then

l𝒟⁢(f+g)≥l𝒟⁢(f)+l𝒟⁢(g)𝑎𝑛𝑑u𝒟⁢(f+g)≤u𝒟⁢(f)+u𝒟⁢(g).l_{\mathscr{D}}(f+g)\geq l_{\mathscr{D}}(f)+l_{\mathscr{D}}(g)\quad\mbox{and}% \quad u_{\mathscr{D}}(f+g)\leq u_{\mathscr{D}}(f)+u_{\mathscr{D}}(g).
Proof.

Theorem 4.24 (Linearity of the Riemann Integral)

Let f,gf,g be Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b], and α∈ℝ\alpha\in{\mathbb{R}} be a constant. Then

  • (i)
    ​

    α⁢f\alpha f is Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b], and ∫abα⁢f=α⁢∫abf\int_{a}^{b}\alpha f=\alpha\int_{a}^{b}f,

  • (ii)
    ​

    f+gf+g is Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b], and ∫ab(f+g)=∫abf+∫abg\int_{a}^{b}(f+g)=\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g.

Proof.

(i) By Theorem 4.16, there is a sequence of dissections (𝒟n)(\mathscr{D}_{n}) such that l𝒟n⁢(f)→∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f and u𝒟n⁢(f)→∫abfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f. It follows directly from Definition 4.6 that l𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)=α⁢l𝒟n⁢(f)l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)=\alpha l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f) and u𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)=α⁢u𝒟n⁢(f)u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)=\alpha u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f) if α≥0\alpha\geq 0, and l𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)=α⁢u𝒟n⁢(f)l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)=\alpha u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f) and u𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)=α⁢l𝒟n⁢(f)u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)=\alpha l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f) if α<0\alpha<0. In either case, l𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)→α⁢∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)\rightarrow\alpha\int_{a}^{b}f and u𝒟n⁢(α⁢f)→α⁢∫abfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(\alpha f)\rightarrow\alpha\int_{a}^{b}f by the Algebra of Limits, and the claim follows from Theorem 4.16.

(ii) Let (𝒟n)(\mathscr{D}_{n}) be as defined above. By Theorem 4.16, there is also a sequence of dissections (𝒟n′)(\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}) such that l𝒟n′⁢(g)→∫abgl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(g)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g and u𝒟n′⁢(g)→∫abgu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(g)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g. Let 𝒟n′′=𝒟n∪𝒟n′\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}=\mathscr{D}_{n}\cup\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}. This is a refinement of both 𝒟n\mathscr{D}_{n} and 𝒟n′\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime} and so, by the Refinement Lemma

l𝒟n⁢(f)≤l𝒟n′′⁢(f)≤∫abf,\displaystyle l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\leq l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)% \leq\int_{a}^{b}f,
l𝒟n′⁢(g)≤l𝒟n′′⁢(g)≤∫abg,\displaystyle l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(g)\leq l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime% \prime}}(g)\leq\int_{a}^{b}g,
∫abf≤u𝒟n′′⁢(f)≤u𝒟n⁢(f),\displaystyle\int_{a}^{b}f\leq u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\leq u_{% \mathscr{D}_{n}}(f),
∫abf≤u𝒟n′′⁢(g)≤u𝒟n′⁢(g).\displaystyle\int_{a}^{b}f\leq u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g)\leq u_{% \mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(g).

Hence, by the Squeeze Rule, l𝒟n′′⁢(f)→∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f, l𝒟n′′⁢(g)→∫abgl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g, u𝒟n′′⁢(f)→∫abfu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f and u𝒟n′′⁢(f)→∫abgu_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g. Now, by Lemma 4.23,

l𝒟n′′⁢(f)+l𝒟n′′⁢(g)≤l𝒟n′′⁢(f+g)≤l⁢(f+g),l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)+l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g)% \leq l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f+g)\leq l(f+g),

and

u⁢(f+g)≤u𝒟n′′⁢(f+g)≤u𝒟n′′⁢(f)+u𝒟n′′⁢(g).u(f+g)\leq u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f+g)\leq u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{% \prime\prime}}(f)+u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g).

But both l𝒟n′′⁢(f)+l𝒟n′′⁢(g)l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)+l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g) and u𝒟n′′⁢(f)+u𝒟n′′⁢(g)u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(f)+u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g) converge to ∫abf+∫abg\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g so, by Proposition 1.7, ∫abf+∫abg≤l⁢(f+g)\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g\leq l(f+g) and u⁢(f+g)≤∫abf+∫abgu(f+g)\leq\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g. It follows that l⁢(f+g)≥u⁢(f+g)l(f+g)\geq u(f+g) and hence, by Lemma 4.13, that l⁢(f+g)=u⁢(f+g)l(f+g)=u(f+g), that is, f+gf+g is Riemann integrable. Furthermore

∫ab(f+g)=l⁢(f+g)≥∫abf+∫abg\displaystyle\int_{a}^{b}(f+g)=l(f+g)\geq\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g
and ∫ab(f+g)=u⁢(f+g)≤∫abf+∫abg,\displaystyle\int_{a}^{b}(f+g)=u(f+g)\leq\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g,\qquad% \qquad\qquad\qquad\quad

so ∫ab(f+g)=∫abf+∫abg\int_{a}^{b}(f+g)=\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{a}^{b}g, as claimed. ∎

We next prove that Riemann integration preserves inequalities.

Proposition 4.25

Let f,g:[a,b]→ℝf,g:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} be Riemann integrable and assume f⁢(x)≤g⁢(x)f(x)\leq g(x) for all x∈[a,b]x\in[a,b]. Then

∫abf≤∫abg.\int_{a}^{b}f\leq\int_{a}^{b}g.
Proof.

By Theorem 4.16 there exist sequences of dissections 𝒟n′\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}, 𝒟n′′\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime} such that l𝒟n′⁢(f)→∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f and l𝒟n′′⁢(g)→∫abgl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}}(g)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g. Let 𝒟n=𝒟n′∪𝒟n′′\mathscr{D}_{n}=\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime}\cup\mathscr{D}_{n}^{\prime\prime}. Then, by the Refinement Lemma (and the Squeeze Rule) l𝒟n⁢(f)→∫abfl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}f and l𝒟n⁢(g)→∫abgl_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(g)\rightarrow\int_{a}^{b}g also. But, for each nn, l𝒟n⁢(g)≥l𝒟n⁢(f)l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(g)\geq l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f) since infg≥inff\inf g\geq\inf f on any subset of [a,b][a,b]. Hence, ∫abg−∫abf\int_{a}^{b}g-\int_{a}^{b}f, being the limit of a convergent non-negative sequence, is ≥0\geq 0 (Proposition 1.7). ∎

From this we can deduce a kind of “triangle inequality” for Riemann integrals.

Proposition 4.26 (Integral Triangle Inequality)

Let f:[a,b]→ℝf:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} be Riemann integrable. Then |f|:[a,b]→ℝ|f|:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} is Riemann integrable, and

|∫abf|≤∫ab|f|.\left|\int_{a}^{b}f\right|\leq\int_{a}^{b}|f|.
Proof.

We first note that, if |f||f| is Riemann integrable then, since −|f⁢(x)|≤f⁢(x)≤|f⁢(x)|-|f(x)|\leq f(x)\leq|f(x)| for all x∈[a,b]x\in[a,b],

−∫ab|f|≤∫abf≤∫ab|f|-\int_{a}^{b}|f|\leq\int_{a}^{b}f\leq\int_{a}^{b}|f|

by Proposition 4.25, so the claimed inequality follows. It remains to show that |f||f| is Riemann integrable.

Let f+:[a,b]→ℝf_{+}:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} denote the “positive part” of the function ff, that is

f+⁢(x)={f⁢(x),if f⁢(x)≥00,if f⁢(x)<0.f_{+}(x)=\left\{\begin{array}[]{cc}f(x),&\mbox{if $f(x)\geq 0$}\\ 0,&\mbox{if $f(x)<0$.}\end{array}\right.

We will show that f+f_{+} is Riemann integrable. The claim then immediately follows from Theorem 4.24, since |f|=2⁢f+−f|f|=2f_{+}-f (check it!).

Let 𝒟={a=a0,a1,a2,…,an=b}\mathscr{D}=\{a=a_{0},a_{1},a_{2},\ldots,a_{n}=b\} be any dissection of [a,b][a,b] and consider, for a particular subinterval Ij=[aj−1,aj]I_{j}=[a_{j-1},a_{j}] the numbers mj⁢(f),mj⁢(f+),Mj⁢(f)m_{j}(f),m_{j}(f_{+}),M_{j}(f) and Mj⁢(f+)M_{j}(f_{+}). There are 3 possible cases:

  • •
    ​

    If f≥0f\geq 0 on IjI_{j}, then f+=ff_{+}=f (on IjI_{j}), so

    Mj⁢(f+)−mj⁢(f+)=Mj⁢(f)−mj⁢(f).M_{j}(f_{+})-m_{j}(f_{+})=M_{j}(f)-m_{j}(f).
  • •
    ​

    If f<0f<0 on IjI_{j} then f+=0f_{+}=0 (on IjI_{j}), so

    Mj⁢(f+)−mj⁢(f+)=0−0=0≤Mj⁢(f)−mj⁢(f).M_{j}(f_{+})-m_{j}(f_{+})=0-0=0\leq M_{j}(f)-m_{j}(f).
  • •
    ​

    If ff takes both negative and non-negative values on IjI_{j}, then mj⁢(f+)=0>mj⁢(f)m_{j}(f_{+})=0>m_{j}(f) while Mj⁢(f+)=Mj⁢(f)M_{j}(f_{+})=M_{j}(f), so

    Mj⁢(f+)−mj⁢(f+)<Mj⁢(f)−mj⁢(f).M_{j}(f_{+})-m_{j}(f_{+})<M_{j}(f)-m_{j}(f).

In all cases, we see that, for all jj,

Mj⁢(f+)−mj⁢(f+)≤Mj⁢(f)−mj⁢(f).M_{j}(f_{+})-m_{j}(f_{+})\leq M_{j}(f)-m_{j}(f).

Hence, for any dissection 𝒟\mathscr{D},

u𝒟⁢(f+)−l𝒟⁢(f+)\displaystyle u_{\mathscr{D}}(f_{+})-l_{\mathscr{D}}(f_{+}) =\displaystyle= ∑j=1n(Mj⁢(f+)−mj⁢(f+))⁢(aj−aj−1)\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{n}(M_{j}(f_{+})-m_{j}(f_{+}))(a_{j}-a_{j-1}) (4.3)
≤\displaystyle\leq ∑j=1n(Mj⁢(f)−mj⁢(f))⁢(aj−aj−1)\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{n}(M_{j}(f)-m_{j}(f))(a_{j}-a_{j-1})
=\displaystyle= u𝒟⁢(f)−l𝒟⁢(f).\displaystyle u_{\mathscr{D}}(f)-l_{\mathscr{D}}(f).

Since ff is Riemann integrable, there exists, by Theorem 4.16, a sequence (𝒟n)(\mathscr{D}_{n}) of dissections of [a,b][a,b] such that

u𝒟n⁢(f)−l𝒟n⁢(f)→0.u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)-l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow 0.

By inequality (4.3) and the Squeeze Rule, u𝒟n⁢(f+)−l𝒟n⁢(f+)→0u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f_{+})-l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f_{+})\rightarrow 0 also, so, again by Theorem 4.16, f+f_{+} is also Riemann integrable. ∎

We know that the sum of two integrable functions is integrable. What about the product of two integrable functions? Again, this turns out to be integrable, but the proof is a bit more involved.

Theorem 4.27

Let f:[a,b]→ℝf:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} be Riemann integrable. Then f2:[a,b]→ℝf^{2}:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} is Riemann integrable.

Proof.

Since ff is Riemann integrable, so is |f||f| (Proposition 4.26), and f2=|f|2f^{2}=|f|^{2}. Let MM be any upper bound on |f|:[a,b]→ℝ|f|:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}}, and 𝒟={a=a0,a1,a2,…,an=b}\mathscr{D}=\{a=a_{0},a_{1},a_{2},\ldots,a_{n}=b\} be any dissection of [a,b][a,b]. Then Mj⁢(f2)=Mj⁢(|f|)2M_{j}(f^{2})=M_{j}(|f|)^{2} and mj⁢(f2)=mj⁢(|f|)2m_{j}(f^{2})=m_{j}(|f|)^{2}, so

u𝒟⁢(f2)−l𝒟⁢(f2)\displaystyle u_{\mathscr{D}}(f^{2})-l_{\mathscr{D}}(f^{2}) =\displaystyle= ∑j=1n(Mj⁢(|f|)2−mj⁢(|f|)2)⁢(aj−aj−1)\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{n}(M_{j}(|f|)^{2}-m_{j}(|f|)^{2})(a_{j}-a_{j-1}) (4.4)
=\displaystyle= ∑j=1n(Mj⁢(|f|)+mj⁢(|f|))⁢(Mj⁢(|f|)−mj⁢(|f|))⁢(aj−aj−1)\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{n}(M_{j}(|f|)+m_{j}(|f|))(M_{j}(|f|)-m_{j}(|f|))(a_{j% }-a_{j-1})
≤\displaystyle\leq ∑j=1n2⁢M⁢(Mj⁢(|f|)−mj⁢(|f|))⁢(aj−aj−1)\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{n}2M(M_{j}(|f|)-m_{j}(|f|))(a_{j}-a_{j-1})
=\displaystyle= 2M(u𝒟(|f|)−l𝒟(|f||)).\displaystyle 2M(u_{\mathscr{D}}(|f|)-l_{\mathscr{D}}(|f||)).

Since |f||f| is Riemann integrable, there exists a sequence (𝒟n)(\mathscr{D}_{n}) of diessctions of [a,b][a,b] such that

u𝒟n⁢(|f|)−l𝒟n⁢(|f|)→0u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(|f|)-l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(|f|)\rightarrow 0

by Theorem 4.16. But then by (4.4) and the Squeeze Rule,

u𝒟n⁢(f2)−l𝒟n⁢(f2)→0u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f^{2})-l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f^{2})\rightarrow 0

also, so f2f^{2} is Riemann integrable by Theorem 4.16. ∎

Corollary 4.28 (Algebra Property of Integrable Functions)

Let f,gf,g be bounded functions Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b] and α∈ℝ\alpha\in{\mathbb{R}} be a constant. Then

α⁢f,f+g,𝑎𝑛𝑑⁢f⁢g\alpha f,\>\>f+g,\>\>\mbox{and}\>\>fg

are all Riemann integrable on [a,b][a,b].

Proof.

We have already proved that α⁢f\alpha f and f+gf+g are integrable (Theorem 4.24) so it remains to show that f⁢gfg is integrable. By Theorem 4.27, f2f^{2}, g2g^{2} and (f+g)2(f+g)^{2} are all integrable. Hence, by Theorem 4.24,

12⁢[(f+g)2−f2−g2]=f⁢g\frac{1}{2}[(f+g)^{2}-f^{2}-g^{2}]=fg

is also integrable. ∎

Let’s denote by L⁢([a,b])L([a,b]) the set of Riemann integrable functions on [a,b][a,b]. Theorem 4.24 establishes that L⁢([a,b])L([a,b]) is in fact a vector space over ℝ{\mathbb{R}}: it is closed under the obvious (vector) addition and scalar multiplication operations. Better still, it shows that the map

L⁢([a,b])→ℝ,f↦∫abfL([a,b])\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}},\qquad f\mapsto\int_{a}^{b}f

is a linear map between vector spaces. Corollary 4.28 establishes, further, that L⁢([a,b])L([a,b]) is an algebra, that is, a vector space with closed multiplication operation (in this case (f×g)⁢(x)=f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)(f\times g)(x)=f(x)g(x), pointwise multiplication).

Summary

  • •
    ​

    A dissection of [a,b][a,b] is a finite subset 𝒟={a0,a1,…,an}\mathscr{D}=\{a_{0},a_{1},\ldots,a_{n}\} of [a,b][a,b] such that

    a=a0<a1<a2<⋯<an=b.a=a_{0}<a_{1}<a_{2}<\cdots<a_{n}=b.
  • •
    ​

    Given a bounded function f:[a,b]→ℝf:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}}, and a dissection 𝒟\mathscr{D}, the lower Riemann sum is

    l𝒟⁢(f)=∑j=1ninf{f⁢(x):aj−1≤x≤aj}⁢(aj−aj−1),l_{\mathscr{D}}(f)=\sum_{j=1}^{n}\inf\{f(x)\>:\>a_{j-1}\leq x\leq a_{j}\}(a_{j% }-a_{j-1}),

    and the upper Riemann sum is

    u𝒟⁢(f)=∑j=1nsup{f⁢(x):aj−1≤x≤aj}⁢(aj−aj−1).u_{\mathscr{D}}(f)=\sum_{j=1}^{n}\sup\{f(x)\>:\>a_{j-1}\leq x\leq a_{j}\}(a_{j% }-a_{j-1}).
  • •
    ​

    The lower Riemann integral of ff is

    l⁢(f)=sup{l𝒟⁢(f):𝒟 any dissection of [a,b]},l(f)=\sup\{l_{\mathscr{D}}(f)\>:\>\mbox{$\mathscr{D}$ any dissection of $[a,b]% $}\},

    and the upper Riemann integral of ff is

    u⁢(f)=inf{u𝒟⁢(f):𝒟 any dissection of [a,b]}.u(f)=\inf\{u_{\mathscr{D}}(f)\>:\>\mbox{$\mathscr{D}$ any dissection of $[a,b]% $}\}.
  • •
    ​

    ff is Riemann integrable if l⁢(f)=u⁢(f)l(f)=u(f), and in this case we denote their common value by

    ∫abf\int_{a}^{b}f

    and call it the Riemann integral of ff (on, or over, [a,b][a,b]).

  • •
    ​

    A bounded function f:[a,b]→ℝf:[a,b]\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} is Riemann integrable if and only if there exists some sequence (𝒟n)(\mathscr{D}_{n}) of dissections of [a,b][a,b] such that l𝒟n⁢(f)−u𝒟n⁢(f)→0l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)-u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)\rightarrow 0 (and, in this case, ∫abf=liml𝒟n⁢(f)=limu𝒟n⁢(f)\int_{a}^{b}f=\lim l_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)=\lim u_{\mathscr{D}_{n}}(f)).

  • •
    ​

    We used this theorem to prove that all continuous functions, and all monotonic functions are Riemann integrable. We also used it to prove that

    ∫abf+∫bcf=∫acf,∫ab(α⁢f+β⁢g)=α⁢∫abf+β⁢∫abg,and|∫abf|≤∫ab|f|\int_{a}^{b}f+\int_{b}^{c}f=\int_{a}^{c}f,\qquad\int_{a}^{b}(\alpha f+\beta g)% =\alpha\int_{a}^{b}f+\beta\int_{a}^{b}g,\qquad\mbox{and}\qquad\left|\int_{a}^{% b}f\right|\leq\int_{a}^{b}|f|

    where α,β\alpha,\beta are constants.
