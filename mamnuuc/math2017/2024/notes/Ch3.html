Chapter 3 Functions differentiable on an interval

Two applications of differential calculus are particularly pervasive in the natural sciences. In one, we use the derivative of a function to deduce its maximum (or minimum) value and the input which produces that value. In the second, we are given information, not about the function itself, but about its derivative (which is often interpreted as a ‚Äúrate of change‚Äù with respect to time).
