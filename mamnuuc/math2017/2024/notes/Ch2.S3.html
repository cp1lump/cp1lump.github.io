Open sets and the Localization Lemma

2.3 Open sets and the Localization Lemma

We will often have to deal with functions f:ℝ→ℝf:{\mathbb{R}}\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} that are defined piecewise, by combining different formulae for f⁢(x)f(x) valid on different subsets of the domain. A simple example is

f:ℝ→ℝ,f⁢(x)=|x|={x,x∈[0,∞),−x,x∈(−∞,0).f:{\mathbb{R}}\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}},\qquad f(x)=|x|=\left\{\begin{array}[]{% cc}x,&x\in[0,\infty),\\ -x,&x\in(-\infty,0).\end{array}\right.

It would be handy if we could deduce information about the differentiablity of such functions directly from their constituent pieces. Luckily, we can, because limits, and hence derivatives, are inherently local objects. That is, if two functions f,gf,g coincide for all xx “close to” a point aa, they have the same derivative at aa. To formulate this statement precisely (and hence be able to prove it) we need to introduce the concept of open subsets of ℝ{\mathbb{R}}.

Definition 2.21

U⊆ℝU\subseteq{\mathbb{R}} is open if, for each a∈Ua\in U there exists δ>0\delta>0 such that (a−δ,a+δ)⊆U(a-\delta,a+\delta)\subseteq U.

We can now give a precise formulation of the idea that derivatives are local.

Lemma 2.24 (Localization Lemma)

Let f:D→ℝf:D\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} coincide with a differentiable function g:U→ℝg:U\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}} on some open set U⊆DU\subseteq D. Then ff is differentiable, with derivative g′g^{\prime}, on UU.

Proof.

Let a∈Ua\in U. We must show that

limx→af⁢(x)−f⁢(a)x−a=g′⁢(a).\lim_{x\rightarrow a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}=g^{\prime}(a).

Let ε>0\varepsilon>0 be given. Since UU is open, there exists δ1>0\delta_{1}>0 such that (a−δ1,a+δ1)⊆U⊆D(a-\delta_{1},a+\delta_{1})\subseteq U\subseteq D. Since gg is differentiable at aa, there exists δ2>0\delta_{2}>0 such that for all x∈Ux\in U with 0<|x−a|<δ20<|x-a|<\delta_{2},

|g⁢(x)−g⁢(a)x−a−g′⁢(a)|<ε.\left|\frac{g(x)-g(a)}{x-a}-g^{\prime}(a)\right|<\varepsilon.

Let δ=min⁡{δ1,δ2}>0\delta=\min\{\delta_{1},\delta_{2}\}>0. Then for all x∈Dx\in D with 0<|x−a|<δ0<|x-a|<\delta, x∈Ux\in U so f⁢(x)=g⁢(x)f(x)=g(x) (and f⁢(a)=g⁢(a)f(a)=g(a)), and 0<|x−a|<δ20<|x-a|<\delta_{2}, so

|f⁢(x)−f⁢(a)x−a−g′⁢(a)|=|g⁢(x)−g⁢(a)x−a−g′⁢(a)|<ε.\left|\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}-g^{\prime}(a)\right|=\left|\frac{g(x)-g(a)}{x-a}-g% ^{\prime}(a)\right|<\varepsilon.
