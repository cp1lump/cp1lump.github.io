0 Introduction

Fundamentally, the importance of mathematics in the modern world comes from our ability to know that mathematical statements are correct. Arguably, one of the mathematical ideas that has had the most impact on the world (and continues to have impact) is that of calculus. Almost every area of science and engineering requires some use of calculus and it has been central to our understanding of the world for at least the last two centuries. However, when we start to delve into calculus, we start to get to some statements which may trip us up (and have tripped up many of the greatest mathematicians of the last two centuries). This can (and has) lead to errors in solutions of equations, mistakes in proofs, entire books that are incorrect… This is where Real Analysis comes in: Real Analysis is the rigorous study of the theory of real numbers and functions on the real numbers. It aims to completely clarify (i.e. prove) the statements that we require as mathematicians.

Here are some statements - which of these is true? (Recall that a continuous function is one that “you can draw without taking your pen off the page”)

    1=0.99999999999999999999999999999⁢…1=0.99999999999999999999999999999\ldots

    If a,ba,b and cc are real numbers and a≤ba\leq b then a⁢c≤b⁢cac\leq bc.

  • •

    Every continuous function is differentiable.

  • •

    Every differentiable function is continuous.

  • •
    ​

    Given a sequence of differentiable functions which get closer and closer together, they will limit to a differentiable function.

  • •
    ​

    There is no continuous curve in the plane (i.e. a curve which you can draw without taking your pen off the page) which covers every point on the plane.

  • •
    ​

    Suppose that a function ff has f′⁢(a)>0f^{\prime}(a)>0. Then, near ff the function is strictly increasing.

In fact, only the first and fourth bullet points are true and the rest are false! When trying to decide which are true and which are false, you may at some point have felt some uncertainty. Indeed understanding which of these is true starts right at the beginning: What does continuous even really mean? What does it mean for a function to be differentiable? What does 0.9999999999999999… even really mean?

So far, you may have been given vague “definitions” such as the “f′⁢(a)f^{\prime}(a) is the slope of the graph of ff at aa”, or (as above) “ff is continuous if you can draw it without taking the pen off the page”. However, when it comes to actually doing anything at all with these, they are useless! For example we couldn’t either prove or disprove any of the above, which is pretty bad given the importance of calculus.

In this course, we will understand more about the real numbers and functions on the real numbers. Starting from just the real numbers, we will see why calculus works and build up the techniques necessary to prove the statements that we need to solve mathematical problems. Beyond this, we will build up proof techniques that will have application throughout your degree.
